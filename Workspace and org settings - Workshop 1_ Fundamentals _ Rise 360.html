Who am I? Identify the role based on key permissions

You'll see below a series of flashcards with permissions that correspond to a Slack role. These permissions are the highest level or privilege possible for that role. In other words, everyone from Guests to Org Owners can join any public channel. But because Join any public channel is the highest level of permission for Members, that would be the correct answer. As well, these reflect default settings. On Slack Enterprise Grid, of course, Org Owners and Admins can pass permissions on to Workspace Admins or even Members. Ignore that complexity for now.

Workspace and org settings

Estimated time: 5-10 minutes

You should already have experience administering Slack through the workspace settings page. It provides you with a central, single point of access to manage members and settings. 


Pay special attention to the following skills:

  1. 1

    Identify which settings are managed from the workspace settings page and org-wide admin dashboard.

Review your workspace's settings

The Workspace Settings page is a one-stop-shop where members can review info about workspaces they've joined. The Workspace Settings page keeps details transparent, so that you'll know exactly what to expect as a member of any workspace. Here's an overview of the information available to you: Visit slack.com/account/workspace-settings anytime to review your workspace's privacy settings.
Read more Slack Help Center

Guide to the Enterprise Grid admin dashboard

Org Owners and Org Admins can oversee their Slack Enterprise Grid organization from the admin dashboard - a one-stop shop to manage settings, members, channels and more for all the workspaces in an Enterprise Grid org. Questions about launching Enterprise Grid? Get in touch with us anytime at success@slack.com - we'd be happy to help!
