Slack plans

Estimated time: 20-40 minutes

Let's talk Slack plans. As an admin, your privileges and responsibilities differ depending on your organization's Slack plan. That's why it's so important to review the plans and their unique features. 

A Slack Certified Admin is proficient in administering Slack on all paid plans and in response to a range of business needs. This means that even if you currently administer Slack on a Business+ plan for your organization, you could just as easily transition into a role at a company on the Slack Enterprise Grid plan. Because Slack Enterprise Grid has unique features for enterprise-level organizations, you should spend additional time mastering its ins and outs.

Pay special attention to the following skills: 

  1. 1

    Match core features to each of Slack's paid plans.

  2. 2

    Identify the Slack plan that best solves for common business needs.  

  3. 3

    Explain how the Enterprise Grid plan is different from other Slack plans.

Slack has a variety of plans to meet the needs of your team. Below we'll cover the primary differences between the paid plans. Any features available in a lower tier plan are available in higher tier plans. For example, all the features listed for Slack Pro plans are also available for Business+ and so on.

Paid plans: Pro and Business+

Pro and Business+ plans offer a single workspace for your small- to medium-sized company or team. Click on the links below to read the plan descriptions. 

The Pro plan(opens in a new tab) unlocks access to unlimited message storage and unlimited integrations(opens in a new tab). The power of unlimited message storage is that Slack becomes a complete knowledge management tool for the entire history of your team, all in one place, just a search away. Unlimited integrations mean that you can completely customize Slack to include all of your existing services and tailor it to your team's workflows. Paid Slack plans also give you access to message and file history older than 90 days, custom retention policies, guest access and access to Slack Connect (formerly shared channels), among other features. 


Some of the upgrades with the Pro plan include:

  • Full message archive
  • Unlimited apps

  • Guest access

  • Google authentication (OAuth)

  • Custom retention policies

  • Priority support

  • Ability to forward emails into Slack

  • User groups

  • Group huddles

  • Custom profile fields

The Business+ plan(opens in a new tab) is great for teams that require advanced security and compliance features like Single Sign-on (SSO) and exports of all conversations in your workspace.


A few ways the Business+ plan is different from the Pro plan include:

  • SAML-based SSO and Provisioning

    Integrate Slack with your existing SAML 2.0 identity provider to ensure secure access to your team. We offer built-in connections to many SSO providers and an option for using your own custom SAML solution. We'll talk more about this in Workshop 4.

  • 99.99% Uptime

    Slack is available around the clock with an industry leading, guaranteed 99.99% uptime, financially backed service level agreement (SLA).

  • Exports of content from public channels, private channels and DMs

    In a regulated industry or have legal obligations to archive messages? Workspace Owners can apply to access their entire Slack message history, including messages from private channels and DMs. We'll talk about data exports more in Chapter 6.

  • 24/7 Support

    Have an issue? We hope you never do, but if you do, you'll have 24/7 coverage and a guaranteed response in four hours or less from our Customer Experience team.


Paid plan: Enterprise Grid

The Enterprise Grid(opens in a new tab) plan was built with larger organizations in mind. Instead of everyone working in a single workspace, Slack Enterprise Grid connects multiple workspaces across your organization.

What's an organization (org) vs. a workspace?

The organization (org)

What is an org? An Enterprise Grid organization (org, for short) is made up of multiple Slack workspaces. An org powers the design, usage, and administration of these multiple workspaces. All members are connected at the organization level members need only one account to access any workspace in the org.

Org Owners and Org Admins can manage settings and preferences for all the workspaces and members within the org. Here are some examples:

  • Security setup (single sign-on, mandatory 2FA, etc.) 
  • Settings and preferences (message editing and deletion policies, channel management, custom Terms of Service, etc.)
  • Member access and management (accounts, user groups, apps, etc.)
  • Billing (company details, license agreements, etc.) 

The workspaces

What is a workspace? A Slack workspace is made up of channels, where team members can communicate and work together. Members can belong to a single workspace or multiple workspaces. 

On the Enterprise Grid plan, the org's workspaces are similar to those on our other plans, but they have several added benefits.

  • Search, direct messaging, and the user directory work across your entire organization.
  • With unlimited workspaces, create focused places to work. Connect the essential people, information, and apps for getting the job done.
  • Multi-workspace channels connect two or many workspaces within the org, making cross-functional communication cohesive and efficient. 

Individual workspaces within the organization are administered by Workspace Owners and Workspace Admins.

Key Enterprise Grid features

We'll go into depth on these features in later workshops. For now, you'll want to know their main definitions and that they are unique to Enterprise Grid.

  1. 1

    Unlimited workspaces with multi-workspace channels to connect them.

  2. 2

    Enterprise-wide organizational directory, search and DMs.

  3. 3

    Centralized admin and app management for all workspaces.

  4. 4

    Slack's Discovery APIs, which connect Slack Enterprise Grid organizations to your third-party eDiscovery or Data Loss Prevention (DLP) partner.

  5. 5

    Admin APIs to help you automate management tasks like creating workspaces or inviting members.

  6. 6

    Domain claiming, which allows you to claim multiple web domains for your Enterprise Grid org.

Paid plan: GovSlack

GovSlack(opens in a new tab) was built to comply with key government regulations for processing and storing sensitive data. GovSlack mostly looks and feels just like Slack, with a few additions and changes to meet rigorous compliance standards, such as FedRamp and ITAR. 

Required reading: Review the GovSlack paid plan(opens in a new tab).

Key GovSlack features

Here are features youll find with GovSlack. You should know their definitions and their unique application to GovSlack.

  1. 1

    GovCloud certified data center
    GovSlack is maintained by U.S. personnel and built so that no customer data will ever leave the U.S. There is a separate Slack instance running in a GovCloud certified data center.

  2. 2

    Slack Connect
    Slack Connect enables secure collaboration between public sector organizations and their external partners via GovSlack instances running in GovCloud data centers.

  3. 3

    Enterprise Key Management (EKM)
    With Slack EKM, you use your own keysstored in Amazons Key Management Service (AWS KMS)to encrypt messages and files and gain visibility into how your keys are being accessed. Administrators can revoke key access granularly, so teams experience minimal disruption. Note that this feature is also available as an add-on for Enterprise Grid.

  4. 4

    GovSlack app directory
    GovSlack provides a directory of curated applications that can integrate with Slack. The directory will denote the compliance status of each app so that GovSlack admins can choose integrations that meet their security requirements. 

  5. 5

    Enterprise-grade administration tools
    GovSlack includes admin tools that allow granular control over user access privileges, data sharing and security, and channel management.
